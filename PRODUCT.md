# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary users are university students browsing and posting campus life updates during spare moments between classes, evening study sessions, club activities, and dorm time. Secondary users are course evaluators and team members who need to quickly see that the platform covers the required visitor, student, social, publishing, and admin workflows.

## Product Purpose

This project is a simulated campus social interaction platform for a Web programming final project. It lets visitors browse public posts, students register with a student ID, publish image-and-text updates, follow classmates, interact through likes/comments/favorites/messages, and lets administrators manage users and content. Success means the required modules are easy to demonstrate, visually coherent, responsive, and polished enough to feel like a real campus product rather than a plain class demo.

## Brand Personality

Premium, energetic, campus-native. The interface should feel youthful and social, but still organized and trustworthy enough for an evaluated coursework system.

## Anti-references

Avoid looking like an unstyled course assignment, a generic Bootstrap admin panel, or a chaotic entertainment feed with noisy decoration. Avoid low-contrast pastel text, scattered emoji-only controls, oversized rounded cards, repetitive card grids, and visual effects that distract from the core posting and browsing workflows.

## Design Principles

Show the community is alive through real feed content, trending topics, active students, and lightweight motion.

Keep social actions obvious and consistent across the homepage, detail pages, profiles, and admin surfaces.

Use expressive color and motion as product feedback, not decoration that competes with reading posts.

Make the evaluator's walkthrough fast: every required module should be visible, discoverable, and easy to explain.

Design mobile-first enough that the same demo works on classroom projectors, laptops, tablets, and phones.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for body text and controls. Keep focus states visible, interactive targets comfortably clickable, and all animations compatible with `prefers-reduced-motion`. Do not rely on color alone to communicate state.
